> Walk through each unit in the order below, with airtime roughly proportional to the amount of content in each unit:
>
> 1. **Hypoxemia vs. hypoxia** — HYPOXEMIA is an ABNORMALLY LOW LEVEL OF O2 IN THE BLOOD. HYPOXIA is a DEFICIENT AMOUNT OF O2 REACHING THE TISSUES — global or local. These are different problems and have different fixes.
>
> 2. **PaO2 staging — under 60 years old, room air, sea level** — NORMAL = 80 to 100 mmHg. MILD HYPOXEMIA = 60 to 79. MODERATE HYPOXEMIA = 45 to 59. SEVERE HYPOXEMIA = less than 45.
>
> 3. **Age-adjustment rule for over 60** — for patients OVER 60, SUBTRACT ADDITIONAL YEARS FROM 80. Example: a 66-year-old has an expected PaO2 of 80 minus 6 equals 74 mmHg.
>
> 4. **Two ways to assess hypoxia** — ARTERIAL OXYGEN CONTENT (CaO2) measures total oxygen delivered to tissues. The ARTERIAL OXYGEN TENSION GRADIENT — P(A-a)O2 — measures the difference between alveolar and arterial PO2.
>
> 5. **Arterial oxygen content (CaO2)** — the BEST MEASUREMENT of oxygen delivered to tissues, the BEST INDEX OF OXYGEN TRANSPORT. It estimates oxygen carried by HEMOGLOBIN plus oxygen DISSOLVED IN PLASMA. Formula: CaO2 = (Hb times 1.34 times SaO2) plus (PaO2 times 0.003). The first term is oxygen in the RBC; the second is oxygen in plasma. NORMAL value is 17 to 20 volume percent (mL/dL).
>
> 6. **CaO2 worked example** — for SaO2 98 percent, PaO2 90 torr, Hb 14 g/dL: CaO2 equals (14 times 1.34 times 0.98) plus (90 times 0.003) which is 18.388 plus 0.27, approximately 18.66 volume percent — within normal.
>
> 7. **A-a gradient — A-aDO2 or P(A-a)O2** — measures the difference (gradient) between alveolar and arterial PO2. BEST done after the patient has been on 100 percent O2 for 20 MINUTES OR MORE. Formula: A-aDO2 equals PAO2 minus PaO2. Interpretation on 100 percent O2: 25 to 65 torr is NORMAL; 66 to 300 torr is V/Q MISMATCH; greater than 300 torr is SHUNTING.
>
> 8. **Alveolar air equation — PAO2** — calculates the partial pressure of O2 in the alveoli. Formula: PAO2 equals (PB minus H2O) times FiO2, MINUS (PaCO2 divided by 0.8). H2O is normally 47 torr. R (respiratory exchange ratio) is normally 0.8. Normal PAO2 varies directly with FiO2 and barometric pressure.
>
> 9. **PAO2 worked example** — for a patient on 50 percent O2 with PaCO2 40 torr and PB 747 torr: PAO2 equals (747 minus 47) times 0.50 minus (40 divided by 0.8). That's 700 times 0.50 minus 50, equals 350 minus 50, equals 300 torr.
>
> 10. **A-a gradient worked example** — for the same patient with a PaO2 of 80 torr: A-aDO2 equals PAO2 minus PaO2, equals 300 minus 80, equals 220 torr — falls in the V/Q MISMATCH range.
>
> 11. **Electrode terminology** — ANODE is the POSITIVE pole, made of Ag/AgCl. CATHODE is the NEGATIVE pole, made of platinum. OXIDATION is a LOSS of electrons by a particle. REDUCTION is a GAIN of electrons by a particle.
>
> 12. **The three ABG electrodes** — SANZ electrode measures pH. SEVERINGHAUS electrode measures PCO2. CLARK electrode measures PO2.
>
> 13. **Lab procedures for ABG analysis — first steps** — OBTAIN the sample; ensure ALL AIR BUBBLES are removed from the syringe; MIX the sample thoroughly; INTRODUCE the sample into the analyzer.
>
> 14. **Lab procedures continued** — as the sample runs through, WATCH FOR AIR BUBBLES OR CLOTS. PRINT, RECORD and REPORT results per local policy. Check results for errors: if inaccurate, RUN SAMPLE THROUGH ANOTHER ANALYZER; if another analyzer is unavailable, PLACE SAMPLE ON ICE; RUN A CONTROL THROUGH THE ANALYZER to check for accuracy. TEMPERATURE CORRECTION may be applied if the patient's temperature is other than 37 degrees Celsius (98.6 Fahrenheit).
>
> 15. **Co-oximeter and spectrophotometry** — co-oximeters use SPECTROPHOTOMETRY: spectro for spectrum, photo for light, metry for measurement. LIGHT is transmitted at specific wavelengths, PASSES through the blood, and is RECEIVED and ANALYZED by a photo detector on the opposite side.
>
> 16. **What light can do** — light transmission can be ABSORBED (optical density), PASS THROUGH (inverse to light absorbed), or be REFLECTED (backscatter oximetry).
>
> 17. **Lambert-Beer law and isobestic points** — the LAMBERT-BEER LAW determines hemoglobin SATURATION and CONCENTRATION. Each form of hemoglobin has its own unique absorption/transmission spectrum. ISOBESTIC POINTS are used to determine HEMOGLOBIN CONCENTRATION REGARDLESS OF ITS SATURATION.
>
> 18. **Co-oximetry and the hemoglobin species** — co-oximetry uses EIGHT light wavelengths to differentiate hemoglobin species. The five species: Hb (DE-OXYGENATED); HbO2 (OXYGENATED); MetHb (METHEMOGLOBIN, Fe-three-plus); HbCO (CARBOXYHEMOGLOBIN); SHb (SULFHEMOGLOBIN).
>
> 19. **SaO2, total hemoglobin, functional vs. fractional** — SaO2 is the PERCENTAGE of hemoglobin BOUND with oxygen. Total Hemoglobin (tHb, g/dL) is comprised of FUNCTIONAL and FRACTIONAL SaO2. FUNCTIONAL SaO2 is the SpO2 equivalent — the Hb BOUND TO OXYGEN, typically taken from a pulse-ox reading. FRACTIONAL SaO2 represents the DEOXYGENATED Hb species (all other forms of Hb).
>
> 20. **Normal tHb values** — adult MALE 13.5 to 18.0 g/dL. Adult FEMALE 12.0 to 16.0 g/dL. NEWBORN 14.0 to 24.0 g/dL.
>
> 21. **Carboxyhemoglobin (HbCO)** — caused by SMOKE INHALATION / carbon monoxide. NORMAL range: 1.5 to 9.0 percent of tHb.
>
> 22. **Methemoglobin (MetHb)** — NORMAL = 1 percent or less. ABNORMAL blood state — METHEMOGLOBINEMIA — is MetHb greater than 1 percent. Causes: iNO in the ICU setting; HURRICANE topical anesthetic during bronchoscopy; NITROGLYCERIN. TREATMENT is METHYLENE BLUE.
>
> 23. **Point-of-care testing (POCT) — definition** — laboratory testing performed OUTSIDE OF THE CENTRAL LABORATORY (at the patient's bedside). Classified as MODERATELY COMPLEX according to CLIA.
>
> 24. **POCT benefits** — SHORTENED TURNAROUND TIME; MORE TIMELY INTERVENTION; CORRELATION WITH PATIENT STATUS at bedside; REDUCED ERRORS related to handling, labeling, and reporting; SMALL SAMPLE SIZE — only 2 to 3 drops of blood.
>
> 25. **POCT equipment — handheld analyzer** — features a CARTRIDGE PORT, NUMERICAL KEYPAD, 50-PATIENT SAMPLE STORAGE, IR TRANSMITTER, BATTERY POWERED, PRINTER, and a CALIBRATION CARD. Plus CARTRIDGES.
>
> 26. **POCT procedure — i-STAT — sampling** — collect blood specimen. SYRINGE with anticoagulant must be tested within 5 MINUTES. CAPILLARY TUBES must be tested within 3 MINUTES. Gently MIX the sample in syringes or collection tubes. Practice sample-handling precautions per organizational policies.
>
> 27. **POCT procedure — i-STAT — analyzing** — FILL the cartridge via the sample well using SLOW STEADY PRESSURE, 2 to 3 drops. CLOSE the cartridge, press on the tab until it SNAPS.
>
> 28. **Quality assurance (QA) definition** — a SYSTEMATIC PROCESS designed to MONITOR, DOCUMENT, and REGULATE the ACCURACY AND RELIABILITY of laboratory measurements.
>
> 29. **Regulatory and accreditation agencies** — CLIA (Clinical Laboratory Improvement Act/Amendments); CAP (College of American Pathologists); TJC (The Joint Commission); CLIP (Clinical Laboratory Improvement Program — MILITARY version of CLIA); CMS (Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services); NCCLS (National Committee of Clinical Laboratory Standards); plus LOCAL OPERATING INSTRUCTIONS.
>
> 30. **Preventive maintenance** — CLEAN BLOOD SPILLS with appropriate cleaning solution to eliminate contamination. REPLACE WASTE BOTTLES. CHECK SUPPLY LEVELS and replace if necessary — wash and cleaning solutions, calibration BUFFER SOLUTIONS, calibration GAS TANK PRESSURE (CO2). Always wear GLOVES AND EYE PROTECTION.
>
> 31. **Non-scheduled maintenance** — replace as required: ELECTRODES, ELECTRODE SOLUTION, ELECTRODE MEMBRANE, PUMP TUBING. CLEAN DUST FILTERS — replace as needed.
>
> 32. **Calibration definition** — equipment is ADJUSTED OR CORRECTED to match the control standards. Performed on electrodes BEFORE analyzing samples to establish the accuracy of results. CALIBRATION STANDARDS are specific blood gas values used to set the machine to read LINEARLY over the expected range.
>
> 33. **Calibration cadence — 1-point** — calibrates the pH, pCO2, and pO2 electrodes to ONE POINT. Performed BEFORE EVERY BLOOD SAMPLE is run. AUTOMATICALLY runs every 30 MINUTES. MANUALLY runs after performing maintenance.
>
> 34. **Calibration cadence — 2-point** — calibrates the electrodes at TWO POINTS (LOW and HIGH). Controls performed EVERY 8 HOURS. MANUALLY runs after performing maintenance.
>
> 35. **Calibration cadence — 3-point** — performed EVERY 6 MONTHS. CHECKS MACHINE FOR LINEARITY.
>
> 36. **Quality control (QC) definition** — a test performed to determine the ACCURACY AND PRECISION of a device against a known. ACCURACY is how closely the measured results reflect the actual value. PRECISION is an index of dispersion of repeated measurements.
>
> 37. **Internal QC (analyzer controls)** — CONTROLS are samples with KNOWN VALUES run to ensure the analyzer is operating correctly. Assures the RELIABILITY of blood-sample values. Values should be within the manufacturer's standard deviation. Frequency: ONCE EVERY 8 HOURS if running an around-the-clock laboratory (respiratory therapy); ONCE BEFORE THE START of a normal duty day, 8-hour shift (pulmonary laboratory).
>
> 38. **Levey-Jennings chart** — a GRAPHIC REPRESENTATION of each control run on each electrode. Control results are MAINTAINED IN STATISTICAL DATABASES and EVALUATED to detect changes in analyzer performance.
>
> 39. **Westgard rules — overview** — SPECIFIC CRITERIA and actions to be taken based on the results of QC samples. IN CONTROL = no action required. RANDOM ERROR = monitor closely. OUT OF CONTROL = must perform corrective action.
>
> 40. **Random error rule** — ONE MEASUREMENT OUTSIDE OF 2 SD — monitor closely.
>
> 41. **Out-of-control rules — Rule of 2's** — TWO CONSECUTIVE MEASUREMENTS outside 2 SD — perform corrective action.
>
> 42. **Out-of-control rules — Rule of 3** — ONE MEASUREMENT OUTSIDE OF 3 SD — perform corrective action.
>
> 43. **Out-of-control rules — Rule of four 1's** — FOUR CONSECUTIVE MEASUREMENTS outside of 1 SD — perform corrective action.
>
> 44. **Out-of-control rules — Rule of 10's** — 10 CONSECUTIVE control measurements fall on ONE SIDE of the mean — perform corrective action.
>
> 45. **External quality control (proficiency testing)** — a system that COMPARES the accuracy of results from a lab with results obtained from other labs. IDENTICAL BLIND (unknown) samples are sent from an outside agency (CAP) to participating labs. Samples are ANALYZED and RETURNED to the sending agency. Results are compared to the known values and results from other labs.
>
> 46. **CAP — purpose** — College of American Pathologists, an outside agency introduced in the LATE 1940s. Purpose: PERFORM IMPARTIAL AND FAIR INSPECTIONS; ASSESS THE STATE OF THE ART in all clinical laboratory practice; PROMOTE LABORATORY IMPROVEMENT through peer comparison; SATISFY REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS of credentialing agencies; GRANT ACCREDITATION.
>
> 47. **CAP accreditation requirements** — QUALITY MANAGEMENT PROGRAM; LABORATORY SAFETY PLAN; DOCUMENT CONTROL PLAN; COMPETENCY ASSESSMENT PROGRAM; LABORATORY DIRECTOR OVERSIGHT DOCUMENTATION; specifies requirements for a LAB INFORMATION SYSTEM. STANDARDIZES the operation of all laboratories. CAP INSPECTIONS ARE EVERY TWO YEARS.
>
> 48. **CAP external quality control** — SYSTEMATICALLY COMPARES accuracy of results obtained from multiple laboratories. Identical blind (unknown) samples are sent from CAP to participating labs. Consists of 5 TO 10 control samples. LIMITS and STANDARD DEVIATIONS are ONLY KNOWN TO CAP. Samples are analyzed and returned to CAP. Results compared to known values and to results from other labs.
>

## Topic 1: Hypoxemia vs. Hypoxia

- **Hypoxemia** — abnormally low level of O₂ in the **blood**.
- **Hypoxia** — deficient amount of O₂ reaching the **tissues** (global or local).

---

## Topic 2: PaO₂ Severity (under 60 y/o, RA, sea level)

| PaO₂ | Interpretation |
|------|----------------|
| **80 – 100 mmHg** | Normal |
| **60 – 79 mmHg** | Mild hypoxemia |
| **45 – 59 mmHg** | Moderate hypoxemia |
| **< 45 mmHg** | Severe hypoxemia |

**Over-60 age adjustment:** subtract additional years from 80. Example — 66 y/o: 80 − 6 = **74 mmHg**.

---

## Topic 3: Arterial Oxygen Content (CaO₂)

- Best measurement of oxygen **delivered to the tissues** — best index of oxygen transport.
- Includes **RBC-bound** O₂ and **plasma-dissolved** O₂.

> **CaO₂ = (Hb × 1.34 × SaO₂) + (PaO₂ × 0.003)**

**Normal: 17–20 vol % (mL/dL).**

Worked example — Hb 14 g, SaO₂ 98%, PaO₂ 90 torr:
(14 × 1.34 × 0.98) + (90 × 0.003) ≈ **18.66 vol %**.

---

## Topic 4: A–a Gradient

- Difference between **alveolar and arterial PO₂**.
- Best measured **after 20+ minutes on 100% O₂**.
- **A–aDO₂ = PAO₂ − PaO₂.**

| On 100% O₂ | Interpretation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **25 – 65 torr** | Normal |
| **66 – 300 torr** | V/Q mismatch |
| **> 300 torr** | Shunt |

---

## Topic 5: Alveolar Air Equation (PAO₂)

> **PAO₂ = (P_B − 47) × FiO₂ − (PaCO₂ / 0.8)**

- **H₂O = 47 torr** · **R = 0.8**.
- Varies directly with FiO₂ and P_B.

Worked example — FiO₂ 0.50, PaCO₂ 40, P_B 747:
(747 − 47) × 0.50 − (40/0.8) = 350 − 50 = **300 torr**.

If PaO₂ = 80: A–aDO₂ = 300 − 80 = **220 torr → V/Q mismatch**.

## Topic 8: ABG Laboratory Procedures

1. Obtain the sample.
2. Remove all **air bubbles** from the syringe.
3. **Mix thoroughly**.
4. Introduce the sample into the analyzer.
5. Watch for **air bubbles or clots** as it runs.
6. **Print, record, report** per local policy.
7. If results are inaccurate: run on another analyzer; if none, **ice the sample** and **run a control**.
8. **Temperature-correct** results when the patient temp ≠ 37 °C (98.6 °F).

## Topic 9: Co-oximetry — Spectrophotometry and Lambert-Beer

- **Spectro** = spectrum · **photo** = light · **metry** = measurement.
- Light is **absorbed**, **passes through**, or is **reflected** (backscatter).
- **Lambert-Beer law** determines Hb saturation/concentration.
- **Isobestic points** determine Hb **concentration regardless of saturation**.
- Co-oximetry uses **8 wavelengths** to differentiate Hb species.

| Hb Species | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hb** | Deoxygenated |
| **HbO₂** | Oxygenated |
| **MetHb** | Methemoglobin (Fe³⁺) |
| **HbCO** | Carboxyhemoglobin |
| **SHb** | Sulfhemoglobin |

- **Fractional SaO₂** — deoxygenated Hb species.

## Topic 12: Point-of-Care Testing (POCT)

- Lab testing **outside the central lab** (bedside).
- **CLIA-classified "Moderately Complex"**.
- Benefits: faster turnaround, timely intervention, bedside correlation, fewer handling errors, **2–3 drops of blood**.
- **Handheld analyzer** features cartridge port, keypad, 50-patient storage, IR transmitter, battery, printer, calibration card, cartridges.

### i-STAT Procedure

- Syringe with anticoagulant → test **within 5 min**.
- Capillary tubes → test **within 3 min**.
- Mix gently · follow handling precautions.
- Fill cartridge with **slow steady pressure** (2–3 drops) · close cartridge until tab **snaps**.

## Topic 13: Quality Assurance

### Regulatory & Accreditation Agencies

- **CLIA** · **CAP** · **TJC** · **CLIP** (military CLIA) · **CMS** · **NCCLS** · local operating instructions.

## Topic 14: Calibration

- Adjusts equipment to **control standards**.
- Performed **before analyzing samples** to establish accuracy.
- **Calibration standards** = blood gas values that set the machine to read linearly across the expected range.

| Level | What | Frequency |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **1-point** | pH/pCO₂/pO₂ to one point | Before every sample · auto every **30 min** · after maintenance |
| **2-point** | High and low point | Controls **every 8 hours** · after maintenance |
| **3-point** | Linearity check | **Every 6 months** |

## Topic 15: Quality Control

- **Accuracy** — closeness to true value.
- **Precision** — dispersion of repeated measurements.

### Internal QC

- Run **controls** (samples with known values) to confirm analyzer is operating correctly.
- Values must be within the manufacturer's standard deviation.
- Frequency:
  - **Every 8 hours** in a 24/7 lab (RT).
  - **Once before the duty day** in an 8-hour-shift lab (pulmonary).

### Levey-Jennings Chart

- Graphic plot of each control run on each electrode, around the mean ± SD bands.
